— Concept

Not a Tourist Destination.
That's the Point.

No crowds. No tour buses. No souvenir shops. Umiyado 30 sits in Mihama — a quiet coastal town where fishermen still haul their catch at dawn and mandarin oranges ripen on the hillside.

The ocean is walking distance. Mountains rise behind you. The Hidaka River runs nearby. And all of this is just one hour from Kansai International Airport.

Your host is a certified Kumano Kodo guide who speaks English, Spanish, and Japanese. He'll point you to the best fish market, the hidden beach, the trail nobody knows — the Japan that guidebooks miss.

For larger groups, the adjacent annex brings capacity to up to 30 guests across two buildings.

— Experiences

Slow Down. Get Your Hands Dirty.

The kind of experiences you won't find in Tokyo or Kyoto. No tickets required — just step outside.

BBQ Under Stars You've Never Seen

No light pollution. The Milky Way is visible on clear nights. Grill under the covered tent — rain or shine — and eat under a sky that city dwellers have forgotten exists.

Pizza from a Handmade Oven

Your host built this brick oven himself. Knead the dough, choose your toppings, and fire it up. Kids fight over who goes next. Adults pretend they don't.

Pick Fruit from the Garden

Mandarin oranges, lemons, persimmons — growing right outside your door. Pick them warm from the sun. No farm visit needed. It's just... here.

Catch Your Own Dinner

The fishing port is a short walk. Borrow a rod, catch aji or squid, bring it back, and grill it. That fish-to-table gap? About 30 minutes. Evenings: projector movies, Catan, and table tennis.

— FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How many guests can stay at Umiyado 30?
The main house sleeps up to 12 guests (107㎡, 4 bedrooms). With the adjacent annex, up to 30 guests can stay across both buildings. Perfect for family reunions, friend groups, and corporate retreats.
What activities are available?
BBQ with covered tent, wood-fired pizza oven (handmade by the host), seasonal fruit picking (mandarin oranges, lemons, persimmons), fishing at a nearby port, board games (Catan, UNO), table tennis, and a projector for movie nights. The host is also a certified Kumano Kodo guide who can arrange hiking tours.
How do I get there from Osaka or Kansai Airport?
By car: about 2 hours from central Osaka via the Hanwa Expressway, or about 1 hour from Kansai International Airport (KIX). By train: take the limited express to JR Gobo Station (90 min from Tennoji), then 9 minutes by car. Free parking is available.
Does the host speak English?
Yes. The host speaks Japanese, English, and Spanish fluently. All communication before and during your stay can be in English. The house manual and local guides are also available in English.
Is it good for families with children?
Absolutely. The 800㎡ garden is a safe, open play area. Kids love the fruit picking, pizza making, and fishing. The full kitchen makes meal prep easy, and the washer-dryer is perfect for messy adventures. Many of our 5-star reviews come from families.
Is Umiyado 30 near any tourist attractions?
Not really — and that's exactly why guests love it. Mihama is a quiet coastal town where local life continues unchanged. The ocean is walking distance, mountains surround you, and the Hidaka River is nearby. For day trips, Shirahama Onsen and the Kumano Kodo trail are 45–60 minutes away.
Can I cook with local ingredients?
Yes — and you should. Walk to the morning fish market for fresh-caught tai, aji, and squid. Pick mandarin oranges and persimmons from the garden. The host will tell you where to find the best local miso, soy sauce, and seasonal vegetables. The fully equipped kitchen has everything you need.
